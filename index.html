What is Hallway?

Hallway helps you build strong and authentic teams capable of tackling your businesses hardest challenges. Request a beta invite here.

High-performing teams are better in tune with one another.

We all feel it. We’re more connected than ever, yet the real genuine connections we have seem to be decreasing all the time. Loneliness is terrible for your health, and terrible for your employees. Employees with work friendships feel more engaged. They’re less likely to leave your company and more likely to do better work.

A few quick questions for you:

  • Do all members of your team know who everyone is, what they do, and what they’re like?
  • How long does it take your new team members to catch on to your company’s culture, stories, and inside jokes?
  • How many of your employees have a best friend at work?

What would happen if your team had deeper connection between its members?

Perhaps there would be more trust and loyalty on team, team members would be more engaged and happier to come to work, and they could feel like part of the team faster - rather than being an outsider for the first few months. What would that do for the health of your company?

Most companies can solve this with a little concentrated effort. How?

  • Promoting connections between coworkers who have shared interests
  • Making an effort to include all team members in team events, regardless of their seniority
  • Reaching out directly to employees to let them know that you care about them and their well-being
  • Sharing stories, getting to know one another, and having a little fun once an a while

How Hallway helps you do this:

  • Weekly questions and team trivia designed to get your team members to get to know one another and find common interests
  • Providing a place new (and existing) employees can go to learn more about what others do at the company
  • Set up short social coffee breaks between team members
  • Team games

What’s the story behind hallway?

After being onboarded to a remote company, our founder found herself feeling socially isolated from the rest of her teammates. Her introversion made it uncomfortable to reach out to people she didn’t know, so instead just did her work, sometimes not talking to anyone but her cat all day.

As happy as she was to have a remote position, she still missed that small group comaraderie felt at more “traditional” jobs. As a way to improve her relationships with teammates, she built Hallway in her free time and turned a good job into a great job. After all, remote doesn’t have to mean isolated.

Who is the ideal user of Hallway?

We would describe the ideal user of Hallway as an organization having between 50 and 200 employees, with a number of teams of multiple sizes. If you have an office, some employees are not always in the office, work out of different offices or floors, and some may work remotely. You’re at the point where company culture may be difficult to communicate - so it’s trial-by-fire for new recruits. You want your employees to enjoy coming to work, and you want to promote a connected team culture.
