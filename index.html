What is Hallway?

Hallway helps you build deeply connected teams capable of tackling your businesses toughest challenges.

How? By finding common interests, playing games, sharing stories, and focusing on real, human interaction.

Request a beta invite here.

Members of high-performing teams are better in tune with one another

…but it takes time and effort to cultivate that connection and care. With an increasing amount of distraction and disconnectedness in the workplace, there’s a decent chance your employees know little to nothing about one another.

Not only is that bad for your employee’s mental health, it’s also bad for the performance of your organization. After all, employees who have a work friend are more likely to be engaged in their work and excited to show up every single day.

Hallway helps your team forge the connections they need to become high performing teams capable of tackling every business challenge set before them.

So, here are a few quick questions for you to see if your employees are connected to one another:

  • Do all members of your team know who everyone is, what they do, and what they’re like?
  • How long does it take your new team members to catch on to your company’s culture, stories, and inside jokes?
  • How many of your employees have a best friend at work?
  • How would your organization change if your team had deeper connections between its members?

Perhaps there would be more trust and loyalty to teams and the company. Maybe team members would be more engaged and happier to come to work. New employees may even feel like part of the team faster - rather than being an outsider for the first few months.

Think about it…what would that do for the health of your company?

Most companies can solve this with a little concentrated effort. How?

  • Promoting connections between coworkers who have shared interests
  • Making an effort to include all team members in team events and communications, regardless of their seniority or position
  • Reaching out directly to employees to let them know that you care about them and their well-being
  • Sharing stories, getting to know one another, and having a little fun together once an a while

How Hallway helps you do this:

  • Weekly questions and team trivia designed to get your team members to get to know one another and find common interests
  • Providing a place new (and existing) employees can go to learn more about what others do at the company
  • Set up short social and professional coffee breaks between team members
  • Team games that are remote friendly

What’s the story behind hallway?

After being onboarded to a remote company, our founder found herself feeling socially isolated from the rest of her teammates. Her introversion made it uncomfortable to reach out to people she didn’t know, so instead just did her work, sometimes not talking to anyone but her cat all day. Sure, pathetic sounding, but it’s more common than you think.

As happy as she was to have a remote position, she still missed that small group comaraderie felt at more “traditional” jobs. As a way to improve her relationships with teammates, she built Hallway in her free time and turned a good job into a great job.

Who is the ideal user of Hallway?

We would describe the ideal user of Hallway as an organization having between 50 and 200 employees, with a number of teams of multiple sizes. If you have an office, some employees are not always in the office, work out of different offices or floors, and some may work remotely. You’re at the point where company culture may be difficult to communicate - so it’s trial-by-fire for new recruits. You want your employees to enjoy coming to work, and you want to promote a connected team culture.
